SYSTEM 01 — HOOK RESEARCH
Every Sunday. 20 minutes.
You don't invent hooks. You observe what stops scrolls and extract the pattern. This is your weekly intelligence system.
THE CORE RULE
TikTok data: 63% of videos with the highest click-through rates hook viewers within the first 3 seconds. The average mobile viewing decision is 1.7 seconds. You don't have time for a warm-up. The hook IS the video.
5-STEP RESEARCH PROCESS
STEP 01
Open TikTok or Instagram — search these terms
"video editing tips" "content creator mistakes" "how I grew my channel" "video editor" "editing before after"
STEP 02
Filter by Most Viewed this week
Not all time. Not this month. This week only. Algorithm trends shift fast.
STEP 03
Watch only the first 3 seconds of the top 10 videos
Don't watch the full video. Just the hook. For each one, ask: What type is this? Did it make me want to keep watching? Why?
STEP 04
Log what you find — build your swipe file
Write the exact words used. Categorize by hook type (see Hook Types section). Keep a running Notion doc or Notes file called "Hook Swipe File."
STEP 05
Identify patterns — not examples
Don't copy the hook. Extract the formula. "I made $10K from one video" → Formula: Result + Timeframe. Now you can write 20 hooks from that structure.
ANALYTICS CHECK — after you post
DROP-OFF IN FIRST 2 SECONDS
Hook problem
The hook didn't stop the scroll. Rewrite it. Test a different hook type on the same topic.
DROP-OFF AT 5–10 SECONDS
Content problem
Hook worked but the video didn't deliver on the promise fast enough. Tighten the first 10 seconds.
Build your hook library over time. Every time a video outperforms your average, write down the exact hook and categorize it. After 30 days you'll have a personal library of formulas that work specifically for your account and audience.
SYSTEM 02 — HOOK TYPES
5 types. Rotate all of them.
The top 3 most consistently viral in 2026: Contrarian Claim, Mistake Warning, and List Tease. But rotate all 5 — repeating one formula trains your audience to skip your opener.
TYPE 01 — HIGHEST CONVERTING FOR NEW ACCOUNTS
Mistake Warning
Formula: "[Your audience] is making this mistake and it's killing their [result]"
YOUR EXAMPLES — USE THESE
"Most video editors are killing client retention and don't even know it"
"This editing mistake is why your videos stop at 300 views"
"You're losing viewers at second 4 — here's exactly why"
Why it works: Triggers self-reflection immediately. Viewers pause to check if they're making the same error. Filters your exact target audience in the first second.
TYPE 02 — BEST FOR AUTHORITY BUILDING
Contrarian Claim
Formula: "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong."
YOUR EXAMPLES
"Good editing doesn't save a bad hook. Nobody tells you this."
"Posting more won't grow your channel. This one thing will."
"You don't need expensive gear. You need this."
Why it works: Challenges assumptions. Viewers stay to see the reasoning and often comment to agree or disagree — boosting algorithm reach through engagement.
TYPE 03 — BEST FOR PROOF CONTENT
Result First
Formula: "I [specific result] — here's exactly how"
YOUR EXAMPLES
"I re-edited this video and watch time went from 18% to 64%"
"This one edit change doubled retention on every video I touched"
"I built a video editing agency from zero in 30 days — here's the full system"
Why it works: Credibility before curiosity. Viewers see the result first and feel compelled to learn how. Proof-based hooks reduce skepticism instantly.
TYPE 04 — BEST FOR EDUCATION CONTENT
Curiosity Gap
Formula: "The thing nobody tells you about [topic]"
YOUR EXAMPLES
"The thing nobody tells you about why your edits look cheap"
"What separates a $500 editor from a $3,000 editor — it's not the software"
"Why your videos look good but feel boring — explained in 60 seconds"
TYPE 05 — BEST FOR DIRECT TARGETING
Direct Call-Out
Formula: "If you [situation] — watch this"
YOUR EXAMPLES
"If your videos are getting views but no followers — watch this"
"If you've been editing for a year and still have no clients — this is why"
"If you're a creator spending 10 hours a week editing yourself — stop"
Why it works: Filters viewers precisely. Only the people you want to reach feel addressed. Better audience = better retention = better algorithm performance.
3-SECOND RULE: Every hook must land in under 3 seconds. That means 10–14 words maximum when spoken. If you can't say it in 10–14 words, cut it down. Spoken hook + text overlay on screen + visual movement = 3x higher 3-second retention.
SYSTEM 03 — SCRIPTING
Script before you record. Every time.
Never wing it. A scripted video takes 30 minutes to write and 10 minutes to record. An unscripted video takes 2 hours to record and still sounds unscripted.
THE 4-PART VIDEO STRUCTURE
HOOK0–3 sec
One sentence. 10–14 words max.
Spoken + bold text overlay on screen simultaneously. Add a zoom cut or visual movement on frame 1. Never start with silence, music, or slow intro. Straight into the hook.
Example: "This editing mistake is why your videos stop at 300 views"
CONTEXT3–8 sec
Why should they trust you on this?
One line only. Establish credibility fast. Then move on.
"I've edited content for creators in this niche — here's what I keep seeing."
CONTENT8–45 sec
The actual value. 3 points maximum.
Show, don't tell. Screen recordings, before/after clips, zooms on the specific moment you're talking about. Keep cutting — no dead air, no pauses longer than 0.5 seconds.
Rule: Every sentence earns the next. If a sentence doesn't add new information or push the story forward, cut it.
CTALast 3 sec
One ask. Never two.
Pick one and only one CTA per video. Two CTAs = zero action.
"Follow for more — I post editing tips every week"
"Comment 'AUDIT' and I'll review your channel for free"
PREMIERE PRO HOOK SETUP
VISUAL LAYER
Add a fast zoom cut (1.05x → 1.0x) on frame 1. Motion in the first frame stops the algorithm's test scroll. Takes 10 seconds in Premiere.
TEXT LAYER
Bold caption on screen from second 0. Same words you're speaking. Syntax Amber (#C25A0C) for trigger words. Off-white for the rest. Always.
AUDIO LAYER
Start speaking on frame 1. No music intro. No silence. Impact SFX on the first cut. Voice modulation — go up in energy on the hook, not flat delivery.
PACING
Cut every breath and pause. Target: no gap longer than 0.3 seconds between words. Watch your edit on mute first — if it's boring silent, add more visual cuts.
SCRIPT TEMPLATE — COPY THIS EVERY TIME
// HOOK (0–3 sec) — 10-14 words max
[Your hook here — one sentence, spoken + text overlay]
// CONTEXT (3–8 sec)
"I've [credibility line]. Here's what I keep seeing..."
// POINT 1 (8–20 sec)
[First point — show visually if possible]
// POINT 2 (20–32 sec)
[Second point]
// POINT 3 (32–42 sec)
[Third point — strongest one goes last]
// CTA (last 3 sec)
[One ask only]
Before you record: Read the script out loud. If it sounds robotic, rewrite it. You're not writing an essay — you're writing how you actually talk. Short sentences. Direct words. No filler.

HOOK SELECTION CHECKLIST — before you finalise any hook
Does it land in under 3 seconds when spoken?
Count the words — 10-14 max
Does it trigger at least one of: curiosity, self-relevance, pattern interrupt, or emotion?
Is it specific enough to be interesting but broad enough to reach your audience?
"Editing mistakes" = too vague. "The cut at second 4 that kills retention" = specific.
Can you deliver on the promise in the video? If not — rewrite the hook.
Misleading hooks destroy retention and trust at the same time.
Does it work with the text overlay? Watch the first 3 seconds on mute — does it communicate value without sound?
Is there a visual movement in frame 1? Zoom cut, colour cut, or motion — something that triggers a pause before the hook even registers.
ONE RULE ABOVE EVERYTHING: Post consistently over posting perfectly. A 7/10 video posted on Monday is worth 10x more than a 10/10 video posted never. The algorithm rewards frequency. Your audience rewards consistency. Your skills improve through repetition. Post.
ENGLISH IMPROVEMENT TIP
Record yourself, watch it back, note every word that sounded unnatural. Rewrite those lines. Your English will improve faster through scripting and recording than any course. 30 days of this = significant improvement.
